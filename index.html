Carlat Psychiatry Fact Sheets are searchable online resources developed to give clinicians the practical, bottom-line information they need in an easy-to-use format. We offer two distinct types of fact sheets: Clinical and Medication.

Clinical Fact Sheets provide concise one-page summaries on various treatment topics. Examples include the diagnosis and treatment of common conditions like major depression and anxiety disorders, as well as less common issues like catatonia or eating disorders.

Medication Fact Sheets are similarly designed one-page summaries, but they focus on specific medications. If you're familiar with our medical fact books, these sheets will be reminiscent of that content.

Our primary aim with these fact sheets is to present information in a clear, organized manner that can be quickly reviewed, especially during those brief moments between patient appointments. While they are not exhaustive in their coverage, they are crafted to refresh your memory on key clinical points—those crucial tidbits you may have learned in the past but might not recall instantly. To offer a deeper dive, many fact sheets come with hyperlinks directing you to relevant articles and interviews on our website.

This is very much a work in progress and our fact sheet elves (see our editorial process below) are busily adding more content on a weekly basis so check back in the future if you don’t see a topic covered.

The fact sheet format is designed to give clinicians easy access to key information. Fact sheets are based on Carlat Publishing's newsletter articles and books by Dr. Carlat, Dr. Puzantian, Dr. Aiken, Dr. Feder, Dr. Capurso, Dr. Hendrick, Dr. Ruffalo, and Dr. Aziz. Carlat Publishing occasionally uses artificial intelligence (AI) tools in various stages of the fact sheet creation process, such as creating preliminary drafts. In all cases, fact sheets are extensively revised during the editorial process by human clinicians and by our board of medical experts to ensure quality and accuracy.
